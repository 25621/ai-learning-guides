# Modality Survey

## Key Insight

Almost every [multimodal](/shared/glossary/#modality) paper can be pinned down by two coordinates: *how* it joins the modalities — its [fusion](/shared/glossary/#fusion-earlymiddlelate) point, whether the streams meet late (each modality encoded separately and only compared at the end), in the middle (encoded separately, then made to attend to one another), or early (turned into one shared token stream from the start) — and *what* objective trains it, whether [contrastive](/shared/glossary/#infonce) (pull matching pairs together, push mismatched ones apart), masked (hide part of the input and predict the missing piece), or generative (predict the next token). Read through that lens, the field stops being a flood of unrelated names and becomes a small grid of recombinations: a [dual encoder](/shared/glossary/#dual-encoder) like [CLIP](/shared/glossary/#clip) sits at "late + contrastive," a vision-language model like [LLaVA](/shared/glossary/#vlm) at "middle + generative," and an early-fusion model like [Chameleon](/shared/glossary/#native-multimodal) at "early + generative." Forcing yourself to read five real papers and write down those two coordinates for each builds the single most useful habit for staying oriented as new models arrive almost every week.
